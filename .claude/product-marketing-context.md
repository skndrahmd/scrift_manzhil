# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-02-26*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Manzhil is a WhatsApp-first building management system for apartment complexes in Pakistan.
**What it does:** Manzhil by Scrift digitizes apartment building management — from maintenance payments and complaint tracking to visitor passes, parcel tracking, hall bookings, and broadcast announcements. Residents interact via a WhatsApp chatbot; admins manage everything through a modern web dashboard.
**Product category:** Building Management System (BMS) / Property Management Software / Housing Society Management
**Product type:** SaaS (B2B)
**Business model:** Fixed monthly subscription — PKR 75,000/month per building

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Housing societies, apartment complexes, residential building committees, and property management companies in Pakistan (expandable to South Asia/Middle East)
**Decision-makers:** Building committee chairpersons, society secretaries/treasurers, property management company owners, building administrators
**Primary use case:** Digitizing chaotic, manual building management operations — replacing WhatsApp groups, Excel sheets, and paper registers with a unified system
**Jobs to be done:**
- Collect maintenance payments on time and track defaulters
- Handle resident complaints efficiently without things falling through cracks
- Manage visitor access, parcel deliveries, and hall bookings without manual coordination
- Communicate with all residents instantly (announcements, reminders, updates)
**Use cases:**
- Monthly maintenance collection with automated WhatsApp reminders and invoices
- Resident self-service via WhatsApp bot (check dues, file complaints, book hall, register visitors)
- Admin dashboard for real-time oversight of all building operations
- Financial accounting and reporting for society committees
- Bulk CSV import for quick onboarding of existing resident data

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Pakistani housing societies still manage operations manually through WhatsApp groups, paper registers, and Excel sheets — leading to missed payments, unresolved complaints, security gaps, and constant admin frustration.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Generic property management software (international tools) doesn't understand Pakistani housing society dynamics (WhatsApp-centric communication, PKR, committee structures)
- ERP/accounting software is overkill and doesn't handle resident communication
- WhatsApp groups become unmanageable chaos — no structure, no tracking, no accountability
- Paper registers and Excel sheets provide zero automation and are error-prone
**What it costs them:**
- Late/missed maintenance collections (cash flow problems for the society)
- Unresolved complaints leading to resident dissatisfaction
- Security risks from untracked visitors
- Hours of admin time wasted on manual coordination every week
**Emotional tension:** Committee members feel overwhelmed and underappreciated; residents feel ignored; treasurers worry about accountability and transparency

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** No direct competitor in Pakistan currently — Manzhil has first-mover advantage in the WhatsApp-first building management space
**Secondary:** WhatsApp groups + Excel sheets — the "status quo" solution most societies use; falls short because there's no automation, no tracking, no reporting
**Indirect:** Hiring a full-time building manager/secretary — expensive, still manual, single point of failure

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- WhatsApp-first approach — residents don't need to download a new app; everything works via WhatsApp chatbot
- Multilingual WhatsApp bot with Google Translate support (Urdu, English, and more)
- Built specifically for Pakistani housing societies (PKR, Pakistan timezone, local terminology)
- Complete end-to-end coverage (payments, complaints, visitors, parcels, bookings, broadcasts, accounting)
- Admin RBAC system — committee members can have different access levels
- Automated WhatsApp reminders for payments, complaints, and daily reports
**How we do it differently:** Instead of forcing residents to download another app, Manzhil meets them where they already are — WhatsApp. The admin dashboard handles complexity; residents get simplicity.
**Why that's better:** 100% resident adoption because there's zero friction — no app downloads, no new logins. The WhatsApp bot feels natural.
**Why customers choose us:** WhatsApp integration is the key differentiator — WhatsApp is ubiquitous in Pakistan, so residents adopt instantly with zero friction. No competitor offers this.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "It's too expensive" | PKR 75,000/month covers everything — compare that to the cost of a full-time admin, lost maintenance revenue from defaulters, and hours of manual work every week. Buildings with 100+ units are paying less than PKR 750/unit/month for complete automation |
| "Our society is too small for software" | Manzhil actually saves MORE time for small societies where 1-2 people handle everything — automate the repetitive work |
| "Residents won't use it" | They don't have to learn anything new — it works through WhatsApp, which they already use every day |
| "We've been managing fine with WhatsApp groups" | How much time do you spend chasing payments? How many complaints get lost? Manzhil doesn't replace WhatsApp — it supercharges it |

**Anti-persona:** Buildings with fewer than ~10 units (too small to justify), commercial/office buildings (not residential), societies that don't use WhatsApp

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Frustration with WhatsApp group chaos, missed payments, unaccountable manual processes, committee members burning out
**Pull:** Automated reminders, professional invoices, resident self-service, transparent reporting, "it just works on WhatsApp"
**Habit:** "We've always done it this way," comfort with manual processes, committee members who control information
**Anxiety:** Data migration worries, "what if residents don't use it," cost concerns, tech adoption fears from older committee members
